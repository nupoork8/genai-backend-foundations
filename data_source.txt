Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) is a powerful technique 
used to optimize the output of Large Language Models. 
It queries an external knowledge base before generating a response.

By fetching relevant documents first, the model avoids hallucinating fake facts. 
This makes RAG architecture incredibly valuable for 
enterprise applications dealing with internal company data.

Vector databases like ChromaDB or FAISS are typically used to 
store these text chunks as dense mathematical 
vectors for rapid semantic search pipelines.
